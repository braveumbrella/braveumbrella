To Own a Dalí

Performances were held on October 19-21st, 2017 at 7:30 pm in the Oregon Contemporary Theatre's studio space

Synopsis

To Own A Dalí is about how the traditional idea of a relationship is changing. It revolves around the relationships of 5 characters as they attend an eclectic music festival, which is roughly based on the Treasure Island Music Festival in San Francisco. The characters are in different types of relationships, but they all respect and embrace each other's personal choices- whether it is to be traditionally monogamous, in an open relationship or have a single lifestyle.

Throughout the show, the characters discuss different romantic relationships through symbolic conversations. For example, you might love a Salvador Dalí painting. You can purchase it and care for it in your home, but can you truly own that piece of art? Or does each person’s unique interpretation of art make ownership an illusion?

In a relationship can or should someone belong only to you?

There are performance pieces juxtaposing the dialogue including music and movement/dance.

Director's Approach

This production was set over three days at a music festival. The festival setting holds two purposes. The music and creative environment served as inspiration for character conversations and additionally, the location of a festival is a place that allows the characters to explore themselves and their relationships through dance and movement.
