Founding Members

David S. Gallagher

dgallagher@braveumbrella.com

David is a playwright and director who is focused on creating new works that involve artists from many walks of life. He utilizes the "trigger" method of creating a performance—emphasizing listening, processing, and reacting in real time instead of focusing on rote memorization and blocking. A graduate of the University of Oregon, David wrote and directed student shows in the Pocket Theatre, including Rolling for Charisma; a one-act play inspired by role-playing games and players. He also assistant directed the University Theatre production of Ecstasy: A Water Fable. David's experience has brought an extreme appreciation for actors, writers and technical artists that create great performances through collaboration and use their skills to take simple ideas and make them marvelous.

Echo Johnson

ejohnson@braveumbrella.com

Echo is a designer and stage manager who wants to give a voice to fellow artists, as well as people in her community. She graduated from the University of Oregon where she studied many different aspects of theatre but focused mainly on lighting design and stage-managing. She has worked on various shows with companies such as Oregon Contemporary Theatre, Free Shakespeare in the Park, The Very Little Theatre, and the University of Oregon Theatre. She finds theatre to be a wonderful medium to bring important topics to light. Echo wants to keep giving people the opportunity have their voices heard in a world where it is so easy to get lost.

Gabe Carlin

gcarlin@braveumbrella.com

Gabe is a sound designer, props designer, and lighting designer. He strives for realism and to create a narrative within his designs. He is a graduate of University of Oregon theatre department where he focused on sound and lighting design. He served as Technical Director for the university’s student-run Pocket Playhouse and designed numerous shows in both the Pocket and on the University’s main stages. In 2014, he won an award from the Kennedy Center American Colligate Theatre Festival for front of house engineering in the University Theatre’s production of Spring Awakening. He now works as the sound engineer for Oregon Contemporary Theatre and designs many of their shows.

Juliet D. Rutter

jrutter@braveumbrella.com

Juliet is a director, choreographer, playwright, actor, dramaturg, and properties designer that works to portray modern, relevant stories on stage in an impactful and striking way. Juliet is currently a masters’ student at the University of Oregon, studying arts administration with a bachelors’ degree in theatre. Juliet used this time to explore multiple areas of theatre, helping better understand the process through which different artists are able to tell one story collaboratively. Juliet has written, directed, and choreographed two shows in university’s student-run Pocket Playhouse and won two awards from the Kennedy Center American Collegiate Theatre Festival in dramaturgy (2015) and prop design (2016). Juliet also served on the student board for The Pocket Playhouse from 2015-2016 as the Prop and Costume Liaison between the board and the University Theatre faculty. Most recently, Juliet has been volunteering on the Arts Integration Steering Committee for the City of Roseburg.
