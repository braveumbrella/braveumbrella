To Own a Dali

Synopsis

To Own a Dali is an original piece written by David Gallagher and Juliet Rutter. It is about a group of people who attend a festival to be free, love each other, and listen to some great music. The piece explores the experiences of being in a relationship without judgement; polyamorous, monogamous, or single.

Director's Approach

The setting is a a hippie music festival. Within the festival there is a variety of music within the rock genre.

The show is broken up into two types of scenes: dialogue scenes and performance pieces. The Dialogue scenes take place between two and three characters, attendees of the music festival, while they are discussing a few different subjects: art, comic books, and travel. The performance pieces happen in between each dialogue scene and are movement/dance pieces.
