Synopsis

To Own a Dali is an original piece written by David Gallagher and Juliet Rutter. The piece is about 5 people who attend a festival where characters explore different types of relationships- with no judgement towards being polyamorous, monogamous, or single.

The show is broken up into dialogue scenes and performance pieces.

The dialogue scenes take place as the characters discuss different romantic relationships through symbolic conversation. For instance, if you love a piece of artwork, you purchase it and care for it in your home - but can you truly own it? Or does each persons' unique interpretation of art make ownership an illusion?

The performance pieces juxtapose each dialogue scene and include movement and/or dance.

Director's Approach

The setting for this production takes place over three days at a festival where varying styles of music are being played. The setting of the festival holds two purposes. The music and creative environment serve as inspiration for character conversations and additionally, the location of a festival is a place that allows the characters to explore themselves and their relationships through dance and movement.
